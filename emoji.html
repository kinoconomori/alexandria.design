Emoji Set

Packaging

Social media elements can be utilized to help cross the communication boundaries created by mental illness.



As someone who has struggled with mental illness, I have found that communication with others is always a particularly stressful and difficult task. It is even more difficult in times when it is needed most, but finding words is near impossible.

Social media elements, particularly emoji, seemed ideal as a bridge for this communication gap: simple yet expressive, and available to nearly everyone. A problem with the existing emoji sets though was the overwhelming number of them, and
I created a set of online surveys asking a few key questions about mental illness and communication, and recieved over 200 responses.

1 ) What words or feelings do you associate with your mental illness?

2 ) What do you wish people knew about your mental illness?

3 ) What do you wish people would do for you when you are struggling?

On the left is a visual representation of some of the data I recieved for question one.
After analyzing the answers, I determined that the most important aspects of communication with mental illness were:

How I am feeling
+
What I want you to do for me



From here, I began sketching out possible icons to represent different feelings and needs.
Everyone experiences things differently and at different intensities, so each expression exists in three levels of color intensity
